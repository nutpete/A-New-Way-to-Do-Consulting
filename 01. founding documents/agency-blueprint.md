# Agency Blueprint

## The Two Coaches

**Lead Coach** — works with the program lead. Reshapes how the work is planned and structured. Operates at the strategic level. Heavy at the beginning and end, lighter in the middle.

**Work Coach** — works with the teams. Facilitates the moments where work happens differently. Operates at the ground level. Lighter at the beginning and end, heaviest in the middle.

---

## The Engagement (approx. 4 months)

### Week 1–2: Reshape the Plan

**Lead Coach** takes the existing plan on a page and reworks it with the program lead. Compresses timelines, overlaps phases, designs intervention points. The output is a reshaped project structure where AI use is embedded in how every phase flows.

**Work Coach** gets briefed on the reshaped plan and the teams they'll be working with.

---

### Week 2–3: The Opening Build

**Work Coach** runs the first session with the teams. Before any research or planning begins, the teams build a rough first answer to the strategic question using what they already know plus AI. This sets the tone — we make things before we study things.

**Lead Coach** observes the teams in action. Debriefs with the program lead: here's where your people are, here's what we might need to adjust.

---

### Weeks 3–7: Compressed Exploration

**Work Coach** is present with the teams — checking in, nudging when someone defaults to old patterns, running short interventions when a team gets stuck. Available but not always leading. Reading the room for when to step in and when to let the rhythm build.

**Lead Coach** works with the program lead in parallel. Are the workstreams moving as expected? Does the plan need to adapt based on what's emerging? Teaching the lead to read the project through a different lens — not "are we on track" but "what is the work telling us."

---

### Weeks 5, 7, 9 (approx.): Convergence Points

**Work Coach** facilitates live working sessions where different workstreams bring unfinished work together. AI is used to synthesize overlaps and gaps in real time. These replace traditional handoffs and review cycles.

**Lead Coach** debriefs with the program lead after each convergence. What emerged? What was unexpected? How does this inform what comes next? Helping the lead see convergence as a planning tool they can use on their own.

---

### Weeks 10–12: The Rapid Build

**Work Coach** runs the build sprint. The teams produce the deliverable together with AI — fast iteration, multiple versions, live convergence. Most intensive facilitation of the engagement. Keeping pace up, keeping things rough, keeping people in motion.

**Lead Coach** shifts the conversation with the program lead: what are you seeing in how your people work now? What's different? What would it mean if they could work like this on every initiative?

---

### Weeks 13–16: Refinement & Evidence

**Work Coach** steps back. The teams own the final deliverable. If the earlier phases worked, the reflexes are running and the teams don't need facilitation. Light check-ins only.

**Lead Coach** is most active. Works with the program lead to build the evidence narrative. What happened here? How was this different? What did your people demonstrate? What does this open up for the organization? This is where the larger strategic conversation earns the right to happen.

---

## What Each Coach Needs to Be

**Lead Coach:** Strategic thinker. Can hold peer-level conversations with senior leaders. Understands how to read a project plan and see inherited assumptions. Comfortable challenging how things have always been done. Doesn't need domain expertise — needs to understand the *shape* of how work flows.

**Work Coach:** Strong facilitator. Can read a room, manage energy, create safety for rough work. Comfortable being in the mess with teams without taking over. Can deploy structured interventions at the right moment. Doesn't need domain expertise — needs to understand *when* teams are defaulting to old patterns and how to redirect them.

---

## The Core Principle

The teams never experience "we're learning AI." They experience "we're delivering this objective, and the way we're doing it happens to develop fluency as a byproduct." The structure makes AI use inevitable. The coaches make the structure hold. The fluency is what remains when the coaches leave.
