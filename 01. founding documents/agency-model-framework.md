# Agency: An AI Fluency Coaching Model

## The Core Insight

AI fluency isn't taught — it's developed through doing. Literacy gives people knowledge of AI. Fluency gives them the instinct to reach for it without thinking about it. Literacy can be taught. Instinct can't. It has to be built — through real work, real problems, real use.

The Counter builds fluency from the ground up through a contained sprint. Agency builds fluency from the inside out — embedded in the strategic work an organization is already doing.

---

## What Agency Is

Agency is a coaching engagement attached to a live strategic objective. A coach (or small coaching team) enters the engagement and works at two altitudes simultaneously — reshaping how the work is planned with leadership, and facilitating how the work gets done with teams. The goal is to deliver the strategic objective in a fundamentally different way, so that by the end, both the leader and the teams have developed AI fluency as a byproduct of the delivery.

Agency doesn't add work. It reshapes the work that's already happening.

---

## The Mechanism: Two Distinct Coaching Relationships

### 1. The Lead Coach — works with the program lead

This is a peer-level strategic conversation. The coach sits with the person who owns the strategic objective and helps them rethink the structure and shape of the plan itself.

**What this looks like:**

- Take the existing plan on a page — the phases, the timelines, the milestones — and reshape it together. Not throw it away. Restructure it so that AI is embedded in how every phase flows.
- Challenge the assumptions baked into the plan: why five weeks for research? Why sequential handoffs? Why does building start only after alignment is complete? These are inherited patterns, not intentional choices.
- Introduce a different planning logic: compressed timelines, parallel workstreams, early convergence points, build-first phases. Show the lead that the plan itself is the first lever for developing fluency in their teams.
- Build in structured intervention points — specific moments in the project where the teams will be facilitated through a new way of working rather than defaulting to the old way.
- This coaching happens in focused sessions, not full-time embedding. Beginning of the engagement, key milestones, pivots.

**The muscle being developed in the lead:**

How to plan and structure work in a way that AI use is inevitable rather than optional. This is the highest-leverage change — because if the lead internalizes this, they carry it to every future initiative. The coach becomes unnecessary. That's success.

### 2. The Work Coach — works with the teams

This is in-the-room, in-the-moment facilitation. The coach shows up at the intervention points that were designed with the lead and helps teams navigate the restructured work.

**What this looks like:**

- Facilitate structured sessions at key moments in the project — the moments where the team would normally default to old patterns. These sessions are designed, repeatable, and don't require the coach to be a domain expert.
- Help teams problem-solve in real time using AI. Not by telling them what to do, but by working alongside them as they figure it out within the new structure.
- Make it feel like less work, not more. The team should experience the restructured approach as faster, lighter, more energizing — not as an additional burden on top of their real job.
- Build the reflexes through repetition. Every intervention point is another rep of starting before you have the answer, building before you plan, working around what you don't know. The reflexes develop because the structure keeps putting the team in situations where using AI is the natural path.

**The muscle being developed in the teams:**

The instinct to reach for AI as second nature. Not through training, not through coaching conversations that can be ignored, but through repeatedly doing their actual work in a way that requires AI interaction at every step.

## The Structured Interventions (How the Work Actually Changes)

These are the principles the coach applies when reshaping the plan with the lead and facilitating with the teams:

**Compress the gap between question and action.** When a question surfaces, the team works on it immediately with AI. No research phase that delays action. The question becomes the starting point, and AI helps fill what you don't yet know.

**Make the first version the thinking.** Instead of planning then building, build a rough version immediately with AI. The artifact replaces the alignment meeting. The team reacts to something real instead of debating something abstract.

**Parallelize what used to be sequential.** With AI, a single person can explore multiple directions at once. Instead of picking one approach and committing, run several simultaneously and converge based on what emerges.

**Replace handoffs with live convergence.** Instead of finishing work in isolation and passing it along, bring unfinished work together frequently. Use AI to synthesize overlaps and gaps in real time. Information loss at transitions disappears.

**Make the unknown a starting point, not a blocker.** When the team hits something they don't know, they don't stop and go learn. They keep moving — using their expertise to identify what matters and AI to bridge what's missing. The old reflex is to stop and go learn. The new reflex is to stay in motion.

**Surface the work early and often.** Rough, unfinished work gets shared frequently — not for approval, but for redirection. AI makes it possible to produce shareable artifacts much earlier, so nothing stays hidden for long.

## How an Engagement Might Flow

**Phase 1: Reshape the plan.** Coach works with the program lead to take the existing plan on a page and restructure it. Timelines compress. Phases overlap. Build-first moments replace plan-first phases. Intervention points get designed into the project timeline. The lead begins to see how the structure itself is the mechanism for developing fluency.

**Phase 2: Facilitate the work.** Coach moves to team level at each intervention point. Runs structured sessions that change how the team executes each phase. Between sessions, the team works normally — but "normally" now means within a structure that was designed to require AI at every step. Coach observes, nudges, stays close but doesn't take over.

**Phase 3: Converge and redirect.** At key milestones, coach moves back up to the lead. What's working? What's not? How does the plan need to adapt? The lead is developing their own instinct for how to read and reshape work in real time. The coach supports this but increasingly the lead is driving.

**Phase 4: The evidence emerges.** The strategic objective gets delivered — faster, differently, with AI woven throughout. The teams are fluent. The lead has a new way of thinking about how to plan and structure work. And there's now concrete evidence that fluency changes outcomes — which opens the door to the larger organizational conversation about what to do with a workforce that operates this way.

---

## Why This Model Works

- **The coach doesn't need domain expertise.** The teams know banking or pharma or whatever the domain is. The coach knows the *shape* — where in any project lifecycle the old patterns cost time and where restructuring creates fluency.
- **The mechanism is the structure, not the person.** The interventions are designed, repeatable, and facilitatable. The coach deploys them at the right moments. This is teachable and scalable — it doesn't require unicorns.
- **Fluency is a byproduct, not the goal.** The team isn't learning about AI. They're delivering a strategic objective. The fluency develops because the structure makes AI use inevitable at every step.
- **It develops capability at two levels.** The lead learns to plan differently. The teams learn to work differently. When the coach exits, both capabilities remain.
- **It produces evidence for the larger conversation.** A real objective, delivered in a fundamentally different way, creates the proof needed to have the strategic conversation about what AI fluency means for the organization.

---

## The Bigger Picture

The Counter is a fast-track to AI fluency — a contained sprint that develops instinct from the ground up.

Agency builds fluency from the inside out — embedded in the strategic work already happening, reshaping how it gets planned and executed so that fluency is the inevitable outcome.

Both produce fluent people. The Counter does it in a protected space. Agency does it in the wild. Together, they represent two paths to the same destination: an organization where reaching for AI is second nature.
