§04 · How it works

How The Counter actually works.

find the people already itching to build something.
PHASE 01 · ~1 WEEK

We partner with you to find the right starting point.

Before we arrive, we help you identify the people and problems that are the best fit for this experience — specifically, those who have something they're working on right now that they wish was faster, better, or simply possible. It could be something for their team, their customers, or just themselves.

What happens in the room

Structured interviews and observation sessions with 4–8 team leads. We use our Spectrum Map diagnostic to plot where each person sits on the literacy-to-fluency continuum — not to judge readiness, but to identify where the highest-leverage starting points live.

What you get

A one-page engagement brief with the specific workflow we'll target, the team we'll work with, and the hypothesis we're testing.

the part most programs under-do — actually building.
PHASE 02 · 2 WEEKS

We build working solutions with your teams in a few hours.

In pre-scheduled sessions, individuals sit down with us and their problem. Working side by side, we help them develop AI fluency in real time — learning how to think with AI to diagnose their problem, explore a wide range of potential solutions, and build a minimum viable product.

What happens in the room

Facilitated build sprints using our Rope of Possibility method — we start from what they already know about their work and stretch toward what AI makes newly possible. Teams use the Tuesday Test: if what they build wouldn't change how they work next Tuesday, we rebuild it.

What you get

Working AI-augmented workflows built by your people, tested on real tasks, with documented methodology so they can iterate independently.

aha moments usually land here — keep notes.
PHASE 03 · 3 WEEKS

They test what they built, and we iterate together.

Individuals take the solution they built and start using and testing it in their real work. A few weeks later, we reconnect to hear what's working, what's not, and iterate on their solution together to improve it.

What happens in the room

Weekly iteration sessions. Teams bring what worked and what didn't. We use the Expertise Flip — examining moments where AI's output was wrong or shallow, and showing how the person's domain expertise was the thing that caught it, refined it, made it actually useful. This reframes the relationship: AI isn't replacing their judgment, it's requiring it.

What you get

Refined workflows that have survived real use. Teams who can articulate why AI helps and where it doesn't — which matters more than the tools themselves.

PHASE 04 · LONG AFTER WE LEAVE

They become PROOF for the whole org.

Multiple people across your organization have built real solutions and developed the fluency to keep going on their own. And you have the proof and real outcomes to take this further — to more teams, and at a bigger scale across your organization.

What happens in the room

We equip your initial teams with the Monday Map — a framework for showing others what changed and why. Not a presentation template; a way of making their transformation legible to peers who weren't in the room.

What you get

Internal advocates who can articulate and demonstrate AI fluency to their peers. The beginning of organic spread — transformation that scales through proof, not mandate.
