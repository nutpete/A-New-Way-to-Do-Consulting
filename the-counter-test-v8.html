§04 · How it works

How The Counter works.

PHASE 01 · ~1 WEEK BEFORE

Before we arrive, we do the prep together.

We pick the cohort together — the people with real problems they’re already trying to solve — and lock in the date, the workshop scope, and the communications that get your team walking in with something real in hand.

What happens in the room

Short interviews with managers and team leads to identify 4–6 people with live problems. We co-author the pre-work email and the intro deck so your team arrives warm, not cold.

What you get

A cohort that walks in with real stakes, a one-page engagement brief, and an agenda tuned to the problems they actually care about.

PHASE 02 · THE WORKSHOP · 1 DAY

We teach AI first — then the Sorting Hat picks the problem.

We open the day teaching AI not as a tool, but as a thinking partner — building the instinct to work with it, not around it. Then each person brings three problems from their day-to-day and feeds them into the Sorting Hat, one of our in-house tools. The hat chews them up and surfaces the one with the strongest pull — the problem worth sharpening.

What happens in the room

Morning: the AI-first frame — how to think and work with AI so it becomes second nature. Afternoon: the Sorting Hat cycle — three problems go in, one problem comes out, sharpened and ready to solve.

What you get

Each person leaves the room with a specific, well-framed problem — and a coin from the hat that gets them into the next machine.

PHASE 02
The hat is surprisingly good at this.
PHASE 03 · THE INTERVIEW · 1 HOUR EACH

One-on-one, we make the solution real.

We sit down with each person and walk them through a structured discussion — all they have to do is talk. Their coin goes into the Concept Generator Jackpot, and the reels land on three starter concepts pulled from their own problem. They pick the one that pulls them forward, we drill into the details, and by the end of the hour we’ve built an early prototype in Claude of exactly what they described — clickable screens, real functions, the whole shape of it.

What happens in the room

A discussion guide runs the conversation so they only have to talk. The Jackpot turns their words into three concrete concepts. We pick one together, dig into the details, then prototype it live in Claude — UI, functions, flow — right in front of them.

What you get

A working prototype in their hands at the end of one conversation. Not a slide. Not a spec. Something they can click through and test on Monday morning.

PHASE 03
Three pulls of the lever. One concept in hand.
PHASE 04 · IN THE WILD · ~3 WEEKS

They use it in real work. We iterate together.

Each person takes their prototype into their day-to-day. Over the following weeks, we reconnect — hearing what’s landing, what’s broken, what they wish it did — and coach them through the iteration themselves. This is where instinct compounds: they stop asking “can AI do this?” and start asking “what should I build next?”

What happens in the room

Weekly check-ins. We run the Expertise Flip — surfacing moments where AI’s output was wrong or shallow, and where their domain knowledge was the thing that caught it. That reframes the relationship permanently.

What you get

Solutions refined by actual use, and people who can articulate why AI helps and where it doesn’t — which matters more than the tools themselves.

PHASE 05 · LONG AFTER WE LEAVE

They become PROOF for the whole org.

Multiple people across your organization have built real solutions and developed the fluency to keep going on their own. They become internal advocates — the ones making this transformation legible to peers who weren’t in the room. And now you have the proof to take it further, to more teams, and at a bigger scale.

What happens in the room

We equip the initial cohort with the Monday Map — a way to show others what changed and why. Not a presentation template; a way of making their transformation legible to peers who weren’t in the room.

What you get

Internal advocates who can articulate and demonstrate AI fluency to their peers. The beginning of organic spread — transformation that scales through proof, not mandate.

MATERIALS R&D A reactivation story

Sarah had ten years of data on polymer absorption. She’d never had time to use most of it.

Sarah had a decade of test cycle results sitting in files she flew past every week. She’d always suspected a non-linear interaction between surfactant levels and particle size — but never had time to prove it. In a single session, she built a tool that made her entire history queryable, surfaced the hypothesis she’d written down in 2019 and almost forgotten, and let her run fifty what-if scenarios in the time it used to take to run five.

DISPUTE ADJUDICATION A release story

Maya could decide any case in minutes. Assembly was eating the rest of her day.

Maya spent most of her time not on judgment, but on rebuilding case files — pulling narratives, rebuttals, and transaction records from three different systems twenty times a day. In one session, she built a tool that assembles the full fact pattern, surfaces contradictions, separates verified evidence from unverified claims, and scaffolds everything she needs for the determination. The judgment is still hers. The prep is what’s been taken off her plate.
