§ 01  ·  Point of view

Our answer, up front.

There are two kinds of work inside a research-and-strategy engagement like this.

The kind AI can compress. Sifting a CRM export. Tagging a thousand support tickets. Scanning the landscape. Clustering themes into a first-pass synthesis. That work is getting faster for everyone.

And the kind that takes conviction. Choosing which three opportunities make the cut. Shaping what the top-three narrative actually says. Naming where the competitive tension lives. Landing the ARR story in the room.

That second kind is where AI fluency matters: the ability to apply senior product judgement through these tools, fast enough to reach real conviction in the time available.

Which is why we're proposing six weeks, not four, and why the rationale is the proposal.

§ 02  ·  Two core principles
Principle 01

The structure is the insight.

Before any agent runs over your CRM or support notes, we design the structure it reads against. Fields. Definitions. Decision rules. That layer is the real intellectual asset. Anyone can run an agent over client data. The quality lives in what you told it to look for. It's how Workstream 01 gets built — we design it with your team, show the work, and hand it over when we leave.

Principle 02

AI does the scale. Humans set the bar.

Agents can process a thousand support tickets, cluster themes, and flag outliers in minutes. What they can't do is decide which three opportunities make the cut, which latent need is actually a wedge, or which competitive signal is a tell versus a headline. That's still a human job. It just moves faster when the scale work is already done.

Workstream 01 Client Signal Synthesis
01

Turning your existing signal into a research compass.

Before we design a single interview, we use your CRM, support logs, CSM notes, NPS, and internal docs to figure out where the deepest primary research should go. Every product area gets attention proportional to what the signal tells us.

  1. A1Source ingest. NDA sign-off. CRM export, support tickets, CSM notes, NPS history, product docs, roadmap materials. Format audit. Anonymisation check.
  2. A2Schema design workshop. 90-minute working session with product and CS leadership to define what "signal" means here. Fields, definitions, anchors, edge cases.
  3. A3Extraction pass 1. Agent-led thematic, friction, and unmet-need extraction across all ingested sources. Every claim tagged back to source.
  4. A4CSM + product sense-check. Working session to red-team the output. Where does it ring true? Where does it flatten nuance? Where is it wrong?
  5. A5Schema refinement and pass 2. Re-run with the sharpened structure. Produces the signal dossier.
  6. A6Research-focus recommendation. Product-by-product dossier naming which areas need the deepest primary investigation, and why.
Output Signal dossier across Reconciliations, Post-Trade Ops, Tax, CLM, and Loan Ops. Research-focus recommendation. The extraction schema itself, documented and handed over.
↳ the workstream where "AI-forward" stops being a word and becomes a structure
Workstream 02 Primary Client Insight
02

12–15 interviews. Observation where stated meets latent.

Interviews are table stakes. Observation sessions — watching clients use the suite rather than describe it — are where stated and latent needs diverge, and where the most valuable findings live. We weight our time accordingly.

  1. A1Target selection. Interview list built from the WS01 dossier. Weighted by signal strength.
  2. A2Interview guide. AI-drafted against the schema, human-tuned for the room. Distinct guides for pre-trade and post-trade users.
  3. A3Client briefing note. For Xceptor outreach. Short — the why and the ask.
  4. A4Live interviews. Lead-strategist led. Recorded with consent.
  5. A5Same-day thematic extraction. Agent pulls against the schema within hours of each interview. Synthesis builds week-over-week.
  6. A6Observation sessions. Structured walk-throughs with 4–6 clients across the suite. Protocol and coding frame designed in advance.
  7. A7Weekly synthesis loop. Every Friday, working themes document updated and shared with Xceptor.
  8. A8Behavioural observation addendum. Standalone chapter within the insight synthesis.
Output Client insight synthesis organised by theme. Stated-vs-latent separation. Observation chapter as a standalone deliverable-within-a-deliverable.
↳ Xceptor manages intros. We manage the research protocol and the rigour.
Workstream 03 Competitive & Market Landscape
03

An externally grounded view — with the AI lens on where the market is actually moving.

Where Xceptor defends. Where it differentiates. Where it doubles down. Triangulated from secondary research, analyst coverage, and competitive signals surfaced inside the client interviews themselves.

  1. A1Competitor set scoping. Direct (Duco, Gresham, SmartStream, Broadridge-adjacent) plus adjacent incumbents and emerging AI-native entrants. Working set agreed with you in Week 1.
  2. A2Secondary research agent sweep. Structured extraction across public materials, analyst coverage (Celent, Forrester, Gartner, Chartis where relevant), job postings, product updates, investor materials.
  3. A3Competitive signal triangulation. What your clients are actually hearing, evaluating, or being pitched — pulled from interview transcripts and tagged against competitors.
  4. A4AI-in-capital-markets movement scan. Scoped tightly to reconciliations, post-trade ops, tax, and data automation — the areas Xceptor plays in.
  5. A5Positioning tension map. Defend / differentiate / double-down, per product area, with evidence.
Output Competitive and market landscape analysis — integrated directly into the strategic opportunity map and, where relevant, into the roadmap implications.
↳ the AI lens is the reading light we hold over everything in here.
Workstream 04 Strategic Opportunity Map
04

Up to ten opportunities. Top three, with conviction.

The most important section of the deliverable set. Most of the conviction weeks go here. A prioritised map is the minimum; the real work is making sure the top three are defensible, differentiated, and ARR-linked.

  1. A1Hypothesis generation. Synthesis of client signal × landscape × AI-trend overlay. Agent-assisted first draft, human-curated long-list.
  2. A2Prioritisation framework workshop. 60-min session with product leadership to agree criteria (client pull, ARR proximity, platform fit, competitive wedge, delivery risk).
  3. A3Evidence pack per opportunity. Quotes, tagged source, landscape pointers, sizing reasoning. Traceable to the schema layer from WS01.
  4. A4Top-three narrative development. What, why now, so-what, why-us, what-it-unlocks. This is the slow work — deliberately.
  5. A5Pre-readout tension test. We present the top-three to Sareena before the leadership deck is locked. Better to hear "we disagree with number two" in week five than in the room.
Output Strategic opportunity map (max 10). Top-three highlighted with deep narratives. Evidence packs. Prioritisation rationale.
↳ this is where six weeks pays for itself. four weeks gets you a long-list that looks prioritised.
Workstream 05 Roadmap Implications & Readout
05

Directional input the product and GTM teams can act on — framed for the room.

Everything points to the 60-minute readout. Given the cadence of your leadership offsites, we design this deck as if it's the artifact that'll make the trip to Beaverbrook — structured to drive decisions, every slide earning its place.

  1. A1Recommendation drafting. One directional recommendation per priority opportunity. Specific. Actionable. Contestable.
  2. A2ARR linkage articulation. Range, assumption set, linkage logic. Written so Craig and Grant can interrogate it.
  3. A3Deck design. Decision-first structure: the ask, the evidence, the tension, the bet. Built in PowerPoint.
  4. A4Pre-readout with Sareena. Working-draft review. Any changes land before the leadership session.
  5. A560-min leadership readout. Live session. Built to drive decisions in the room.
Output Stakeholder readout deck (PowerPoint). Roadmap implications document. Meeting-ready for the CPO-vacancy room — and the rest of the ELT.
↳ we assume this deck is the leadership-offsite artifact. if it's something else, we'll reshape accordingly.

§ 05  ·  The plan

Plan on a page.

Two kinds of work run through every week. The compression — agent-led extraction, landscape scans, same-day thematic pulls, synthesis at scale. And the conviction — choosing what matters, shaping the top-three narrative, pressure-testing before the room. These aren't two phases. They happen in parallel across the six weeks. The density shifts: compression is heavier early, conviction heavier late. But both are live the whole time.
