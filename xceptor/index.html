AI-Forward Product Research & Strategy Proposal.

Prepared for Xceptor Limited Sareena Brookshire · Chief Product Officer
By The Counter Fred Sham & Drew Castellaw

Your RFP and FY26 H2 priorities point to a company with a clear destination, real foundations to build on, and a specific set of gaps this work is meant to help close.

  1. 01

    You know where you're going, with a clear target of £100M in ARR, more than 5% market share, and over 100 clients.

  2. 02

    You've got a clear window to accelerate, moving from a flat £44M in FY25 to £50M and £20M EBITDA in FY26.

  3. 03

    You've placed your bet on five solutions to carry the growth, spanning Reconciliations, Post-Trade Operations, Tax, Client Lifecycle Management, and Loan Operations.

  4. 04

    Your platform is a genuine strength, and the next step is making sure the user experience, capacity, and upgrade path match the depth you've built underneath.

  5. 05

    Your clients have moved the bar, expecting out-of-the-box solutions and a UX that matches the depth of the platform underneath, and the distance between that expectation and today's experience is where renewal risk lives.

  6. 06

    AI is the next advantage you have to earn, but early work hasn't scaled and expectations are outpacing delivery. Pinpointing where to focus AI effort across the roadmap is, in part, what this work should help answer.

Here's what we'll partner with you to deliver by the end of this project.

Nº 01

A prioritized view of the biggest opportunities across the product suite

Top three called out clearly, with the reasoning behind each one laid out in plain terms.

Nº 02

A real understanding of what your clients need

What they tell you directly, and what you can only see by watching them work.

Nº 03

A clear picture of how you stack up against competitors

Where you're ahead, where you're behind, and where there's room nobody else is claiming.

Nº 04

Specific recommendations for the roadmap

Each tied to the revenue it can unlock and how it connects to your £50M and £100M targets.

Nº 05

A readout that moves leadership from discussion to decision

In a single session, so the work leaves the room as committed action rather than open questions.

Our proposed approach pairs AI-assisted research with strategic rigour to get to a sharper strategy in less time, not just the same strategy faster.

  1. 01
    Principle

    Start with (potential) answers

    Traditional discovery withholds a point of view until the evidence is in. We start with one. In Week 1 we put forward our best answers based on the data, prior work, and informed judgement we have on day one, then spend the engagement testing which hold up and which don't. Committing early is what makes the later work sharp.

  2. 02
    Principle

    Insight has a half-life

    A finding from a Tuesday client interview is most useful on Wednesday, not in a final readout. We treat synthesis as something that happens in hours, so the questions we ask as we go are sharper than the ones we started with. Stockpiling insight for a big reveal is a relic of when synthesis needed to take more time.

  3. 03
    Principle

    Read everything, focus where it counts

    We work with AI to read every support ticket, deal note, and churn call in the data you share. That full-surface view is what makes the client conversations and product walkthroughs land on what matters, rather than where we happened to look.

  4. 04
    Principle

    Two operators, not two consultants

    We're a pair that works with AI fluency rather than a small team doing things by hand. That changes what two people can credibly take on, and it's why our output beats what a ten-person engagement would deliver.

Our proposed “plan on a page”

Weeks 1 to 3

01

Discovery & Data Immersion

  • One-on-one stakeholder interviews with up to 6 leaders across product, GTM, and CS
  • Internal stakeholder interviews to surface where the org sees risk, opportunity, and friction
  • AI-accelerated analysis of the CRM export, support and CS logs, ticket themes, renewal and expansion notes, CSM assessments, NPS, usage data, and roadmap docs to identify which products or areas warrant the deepest client research focus
02

Client Research Sprint

  • 5–8 client interviews and 2–3 observation sessions or product walkthroughs
  • Sample weighted toward the segments identified in Week 1, prioritized by revenue, margin, and growth potential
  • Transcripts processed with AI as the sprint runs, so each interview sharpens the next rather than waiting for end-of-sprint analysis
  • Insights organized into themes that clarify current perceptions, product value, unmet needs, and growth opportunities
03

Competitive & Market Landscape

  • AI-accelerated scan building on Xceptor’s existing competitive intel, broadening the field across direct competitors, adjacent incumbents, and AI-native entrants
  • Publicly available data analysed in depth and cross-compared against Xceptor’s product to surface where the real gaps sit
  • Competitive analysis framed as defend / differentiate / double-down across the Xceptor suite
04

Initial Hypothesis of Opportunity Map

  • Working hypotheses developed continuously as signal comes in across all three streams, each triangulated against discovery data, client research, and competitive landscape rather than any single stream in isolation
  • Hypotheses proven, disproven, or reshaped in real time as new evidence lands
  • Surviving set carried into the Storytelling & Working Session as inputs to the draft Strategic Opportunity Map

Weeks 4 to 6

05

Storytelling & Strategic Opportunity Map Working Session

  • Mid-point working session with Xceptor leadership to pressure-test direction and iterate the Strategic Opportunity Map before roadmap work begins
  • Client Insight Synthesis presented as the major themes coming out of the research: current perceptions of Xceptor, where the product is creating value, unmet needs, and where future growth could come from
  • Live AI red teaming that challenges our framing of each opportunity and surfaces counter-evidence from the research for the room to build on, beat back, or kill
  • Synthetic AI personas of Xceptor’s customer segment(s) used in the room to stress-test how each segment would receive, adopt, or push back on a relevant opportunity
  • Up to 10 opportunities iterated in the room, with the top 3 prioritized based on client signal, competitive position, and the broader market shifts surfaced through our primary and secondary research
  • The three prioritized opportunities are what we carry forward into the Product Roadmap Implications work that follows
06

Product Roadmap Implications

  • Directional roadmap recommendation for each of the 3 priority opportunities
  • Time permitting, AI-generated visual prototypes (non-functional UI sketches or experience flows) used to bring harder-to-describe roadmap implications to life and accelerate the conversation
  • A qualitative ARR narrative connecting each recommendation to where and how it could create commercial value, anchoring the roadmap in business outcome rather than product logic alone
  • Roadmap implications framed as decisions to make, not options to weigh
07

Stakeholder Readout

  • 60-minute facilitated working session with Xceptor product leadership
  • Active deliberation in the room across the 3 priority opportunity areas and their associated product roadmap implications
  • Built to land a decision on where Xceptor focuses across now, near, and next
